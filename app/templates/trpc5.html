One potential site of action for TRPC5 inhibitor-related analgesia is peripheral sensory neurons of dorsal root and trigeminal ganglia To identify subclasses of DRG Neurons that express TRPC5, we performed RNAscope in situ hybridization on tissues isolated from uninjured mice TRPC5 was expressed in 15% of mouse DRG neurons, restricted to small-diameter, P2rx3-expressing unmyelinated afferents and larger-diameter, neurofilament 200 (NF200) expressing myelinated afferents (adelta and abeta fibers). To specifically assess whether peripheral TPRC5 contributes to neuronal mechanical hypersensitivity, ex vivo recordings of tibial nerve C fibers were performed in wild-type and C5KO tissues isolated 7 days after CFA injection. AC1903 treatment reduced behavioral mechanical allodynia observed in SCD mice, as well as decreased mechanical sensitivity in isolated DRG neurons, even after isolated from LPC-rich native environment, suggesting TRPC5 activity may persist even in apparent absence of endogenous lipid agonist We performed RNAscope in situ hybridization to characterize TRPC5 expression in human DRG neurons TRPC5 expression was observed in 75% of human DRG neurons, particularly enriched in medium- and small-diameter neurons ~80% could be classified as nociceptors, coexpressing either calcitonin gene-related peptide CGRP (CALCA) or P2X3R Spared tibial nerve injury - sural and common peroneal branches were ligated; tibial nerve was not ligated or cut DRG and TG neuronal cultures. Mice were euthanized, and sensory neurons were isolated from bilateral lumbar 1 to 6 ganglia or the bilateral TG; culturing procedures were identical for both tissues. Ganglia were incubated with collagenase type IV (1 mg/ml) for 40 min at 37°C and 5% CO2 and then 0.05% trypsin for 45 min. Ganglia were mechanically dissociated and plated onto laminin-coated glass coverslips. Two hours after plating, neurons were fed with Dulbecco’s modified Eagle’s medium (DMEM)/Ham’s F12 medium supplemented with 10% heat-inactivated horse serum, 2 mM L-glutamine, 1% glucose, penicillin (100 U/ml), and streptomycin (100 μg/ml). Neurons grew overnight at 37°C and 5% CO2.
